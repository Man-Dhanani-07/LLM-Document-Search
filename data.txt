The Indian cricket team, also known as Team India, represents India in international cricket. Governed by the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI), it is a full member of the International Cricket Council (ICC) with Test, One Day International (ODI), and Twenty20 International (T20I) status.

Over the years, the team has achieved significant success, winning multiple ICC tournaments, including the 1983 Cricket World Cup, the 2007 ICC T20 World Cup, and the 2011 Cricket World Cup. India also won the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy and has been a dominant force in Test cricket, holding the ICC Test mace multiple times.

Legendary cricketers like Sachin Tendulkar, Kapil Dev, MS Dhoni, Virat Kohli, and Rohit Sharma have played key roles in India's cricketing history. The team is known for its passionate fanbase and strong rivalries, particularly against Pakistan, Australia, and England.

The Indian Premier League (IPL) has further strengthened Indian cricket, producing world-class players. Currently, under the leadership of Rohit Sharma (ODIs & Tests) and Hardik Pandya (T20Is), Team India continues to be a powerhouse in world cricket.
